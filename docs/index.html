Versus the ranking — surface-weighted Elo beats the official ATP/WTA ranking on held-out matches (ATP 0.6231 vs 0.6255 log-loss; WTA 0.6234 vs 0.6473). Versus the market — on main-tour events the de-vigged Pinnacle close is sharper, and a naive value strategy returns negative ROI and negative CLV. That's the honest result: a pure-ratings model with no serve, injury or matchup signal should not beat the closing line where the market is efficient. Edge is expected in lower tiers (Challenger/ITF) and early rounds — not tested by this main-tour odds source.

How to read this

Every match in tour history is replayed in order, updating each player's overall and per-surface Elo. A dynamic K-factor lets newcomers move fast and veterans stay stable; an inactivity decay keeps ratings current through injuries and off-seasons. A match probability is the logistic of the blended rating gap. The projection Monte-Carlos the bracket to get each player's path to the final; the scorecard's market column joins bookmaker closing odds and measures closing-line value (CLV) — the honest measure, since single matches are high variance.
